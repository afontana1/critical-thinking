# Critical Questions for Course 1: Language, Ignorance, and Epistemic Virtue

## Purpose of this Question Bank

These questions are designed to provoke critical thinking across the course **Language, Ignorance, and Epistemic Virtue**. They are not merely comprehension questions. They are meant to help students examine how intellectual character, language, social trust, ignorance, reasoning, and skepticism shape inquiry.

The questions can be used for class discussion, journals, essays, debates, reading responses, capstone projects, peer dialogue, or self-assessment. Many questions intentionally cross unit boundaries because the course itself treats critical thinking as an integrated practice involving character, language, cognition, social life, and evidence.
